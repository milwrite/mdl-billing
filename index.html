Claim routing pathways for Muhlbauer Dermatopathology

This site demonstrates how rejected, underpaid, or patient-responsibility claims can move through a clearer decision system. The examples below are synthetic claims, built from the interview transcript, the revenue-cycle memo, and general pathology / laboratory billing research.

What this POC demonstrates

  • Three core destinations: rebill, patient ledger, collections
  • How deductible, wrong insurance, inactive coverage, and low-pay scenarios branch differently
  • Where manager review and write-down logic may appear

Important note

No real patient data appears here. Every claim is synthetic. This is a design and logic prototype, not a live billing system.

The highlighted path shows the likely handling path for the selected claim. This visual model intentionally simplifies the real world so that the branching logic becomes legible.

Research patterns reflected here

  • Front-end eligibility and insurance verification reduce downstream denial work.
  • Labs often separate true denials from patient-responsibility balances like deductible or co-insurance.
  • Mature RCM teams categorize denials and route them by rule, not just by staff memory.
  • High-dollar patient balances often need manager review, payment-plan options, or partial write-down logic.

What this still does not know

  • Exact internal thresholds for write-off, hardship, or settlement
  • Which denial categories are most common by dollars, not just by anecdotes
  • Exact payer-specific filing and appeal deadlines
  • The full rejection taxonomy actually used by staff today
